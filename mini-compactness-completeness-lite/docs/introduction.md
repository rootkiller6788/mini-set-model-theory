# Introduction to Compactness and Completeness

The compactness theorem and Gödel's completeness theorem are foundational results in model theory.

## Compactness Theorem

If every finite subset of a theory T is satisfiable, then T itself is satisfiable.

## Completeness Theorem

Gödel's completeness theorem: T ⊨ φ ⇒ T ⊢ φ — semantic consequence implies syntactic provability.

## Löwenheim-Skolem Theorems

- Downward LS: Every structure has a countable elementary substructure.
- Upward LS: Every infinite structure has arbitrarily large elementary extensions.

## Lindström's Theorem

First-order logic is the maximal logic satisfying both compactness and downward Löwenheim-Skolem.
