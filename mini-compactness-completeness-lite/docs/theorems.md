# Theorems of Model Theory

## Core Theorems

- **Compactness Theorem**: Finite satisfiability implies satisfiability.
- **Completeness Theorem**: Semantic entailment implies syntactic provability.
- **Downward Löwenheim-Skolem**: Countable elementary substructures exist.
- **Upward Löwenheim-Skolem**: Arbitrarily large elementary extensions exist.
- **Lindström's Theorem**: Characterizes first-order logic.

## Classification Theorems

- **Morley's Categoricity Theorem**: κ-categorical in some κ > |L| implies categorical in all uncountable cardinals.
- **Baldwin-Lachlan Theorem**: ℵ₀-categorical theories have either 1 or countably many countable models.
- **Vaught's Never-Two**: A complete theory cannot have exactly 2 countable models.

## Main Gap

- **Shelah's Main Gap**: I(T,ℵα) = 2^ℵα or I(T,ℵα) < ℶω₁ for all α.
